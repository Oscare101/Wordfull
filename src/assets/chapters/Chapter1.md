# 🧠 Chapter 1

## How Memory Works

---

> 💡 **Key Idea**  
> We don’t forget because we are bad at learning.  
> We forget because we use the wrong method.

---

## 🧩 Types of Memory

### 🟡 Short-term memory

> ⚡ **Fast but weak**

- Holds information for a few seconds
- Can store only **5–9 items**
- Example: remembering a phone number

> ❗ Most people try to memorize words here — and fail

---

### 🟠 Medium-term memory

> 🔄 **Temporary storage**

- Lasts from minutes to hours
- Needs repetition

---

### 🟢 Long-term memory

> 🏆 **What we actually want**

- Stores information for days, months, years
- Strong and stable

> 🎯 **Goal:** move words  
> from short-term → long-term memory

---

## 🎧 How the Brain Remembers

> 🧠 The brain prefers certain types of information

---

### 👂 Auditory memory (hearing)

- Listening and repeating
- Works only for small amounts

> ⚠️ Weak for learning many words

---

### 👁️ Visual memory (images)

- Pictures, colors, shapes
- Much stronger than text

---

### 🧭 Spatial memory (places)

- Locations, rooms, paths
- Very natural for the brain

> 🏠 Example:  
> You always know where things are in your room

---

> 💡 **Important Insight**  
> The brain remembers **places and images better than words**

---

## ❌ Why Traditional Learning Fails

> 📉 Most people learn like this:

- reading words again
- repeating them

> ❗ Problem:  
> The brain is not designed to remember plain text

---

## 🏰 The Better Way

> 🚀 Use how your brain naturally works

Instead of memorizing words directly:

- 🎭 Turn words into images
- 🏠 Place them in locations

---

## 💡 Why This Method Works

> 🧩 It combines multiple memory systems:

- 👁️ Visual memory
- 🧭 Spatial memory
- 🎭 Imagination

---

> 🔥 **Result:**  
> Strong, stable, long-term memory
